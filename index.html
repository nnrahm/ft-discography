Journey

Formed in 1973, Journey is a popular American rock band from San Fransisco, California. Their style can be classified as soft rock and progressive rock. Journey was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2017. Below are a few of their albums which were produced during the band's new musical direction (and rising popularity) between 1977 - 1983.

Members

Steve Perry

Steve Perry was the band's lead vocalist during their most popular years. Their album, "Infinity," was Perry's debut with Journey in 1978. Perry didn't just make Journey big, he made them huge. In fact, with two public figures in the group, their management company would recieve 600 pieces of fan mail per day. After touring hard with Journey for 9 years, Perry left the band in 1987, halfway through their Raised on Radio Tour in 1987. His last show was in Anchorage before he left for home in Hanford, California.

Steve Smith

A former roadie for the band, Steve Smith joined Journey in October of 1978 as their new drummer. Steve began learning how to play the drums at the age of 9, which can give you an idea of how his skills measure against others within the industry. In 1985, Steve left Journey to pursue his own passions, jass, and to build on his drumming career. He has played on many hits with other artists such as Bryan Adams, and Mariah Carey. Steve re-joined the band in 2017 and still plays for them today.

Ross Valory

Ross Valory was Journey's original bassist. In addition to bass, Ross also played clarinet, bass clarinet, and the guitar. Ross has played on all of Journey's albums to date, excluding their Raised on Radio album sessions and its supporting tour. When playing the bass guitar, he uses a technique he calls "Nashville Tuning," where you re-arrange the bass strings to a B-E-A-D pattern instead of the usual E-A-D-G.

Neal Schon

An original founding member of Journey, Neal Schon is a guitarist, songwriter, and vocalist. He was also a member of Santana prior to his career with Journey. Neal is from our very own state, born at the Tinker Air Force Base located in Oklahoma. Neal picked up a guitar around the age of five. He joined Santana as a teen until 1973 when he co-founded Journey with Gregg Rolie. Currently, Neal suffers from tinnitus, which is common in musicians who have extensively toured in their career.

Gregg Rolie

A founding member, Gregg Rolie was Journey's keyboardist and vocalist. Rolie is a two-time inductee of the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. He was Journey's keyboardest for their first six albums, and lead vocalist in two others. After Steve Perry joined the group, he sang co-lead vocals on several songs in other albums. Rolie left at the end of 1980 and currently plays with his Greg Rolie Band and Ringo Starr & his All-Starr Band.
